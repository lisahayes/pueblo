Do You Have a Slab Leak? Pueblo Slab Leak Risk Checker

A slab leak is a failure in a supply line running under your home's concrete foundation. Because the pipe is buried, the leak shows only through secondary signs: a higher water bill, sounds of running water with all fixtures off, or warm spots on the floor above a hot-water line break. None of these signs are certain proof on their own, but together they indicate how likely an active slab leak is.

This checker scores the signs you are experiencing to give a risk level. It is a guide to help you decide whether to call for professional detection, not a diagnosis. Professional slab leak detection uses acoustic and thermal equipment that locates the pipe break precisely before any concrete is opened. If you want to first confirm water is moving through your meter with all fixtures off, use the Hidden Water Leak Detector tool.

Answer Each Question

  • Unexplained spike in your water bill with no change in water use
  • Sound of running water when every faucet and appliance is off
  • Warm or hot spots on the floor with no radiant heating system
  • Your water meter keeps moving with all water fixtures shut off
  • Damp, warped, or buckling flooring, or a musty smell with no visible source
  • Low water pressure throughout the whole home
  • New cracks in walls or floors, or doors that suddenly stick
  • Unusually wet or extra-green patch of yard near the foundation
  • Home is over 20 years old with original copper or galvanized pipes
